When should you call an emergency plumber?

There's a good chance you've got a plumbing problem when you notice flooding. Property damage caused by flooding can affect both personal belongings and the structure of your home. Furthermore, your water bill will be high, in light of this, it's important to resolve the issue as soon as possible.

In the event that your taps will not turn off, there is a possible flood risk. To minimise any damage, turn off your water supply first if necessary. You should then call an emergency plumber to find out what the problem is.

Blockages in toilets can also be classified as a plumbing emergency, as they can pose a health risk. It's also not a viable option to wait until the next day to call a plumber if you only have one toilet on at your property.

You'll want to call a plumber immediately if any of your pipes have been damaged or burst. Water seeping around your pipes and no water coming out of your taps are signs that your pipes have burst. The burst pipe can be fixed quickly and effectively by an emergency plumber, or it can be replaced if necessary.

Ensure that your water comes out clear, if it looks brown or murky, it may not be safe to drink. The best thing you can do is call a plumber to inspect your pipes. Drinking or washing with the water is not recommended.

In the event that your water supply has stopped for some reason, you should get an emergency plumber out as soon as possible to diagnose the problem and fix it.
Water is essential to all of us, so this is definitely a plumbing emergency. Check with your neighbours to make sure this isn't a problem with the water supply first, as you may need to contact your water provider.
